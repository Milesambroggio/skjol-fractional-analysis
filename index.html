Share Appreciation — Repeat Sales

22 repeat-sale pairs from Colorado resort fractional programs (Vail, Beaver Creek, Steamboat, Aspen/Snowmass): the same share sold twice. Because the identical fraction of the identical residence trades in both sales, share size, property, and program all cancel out: this is the cleanest read on fractional appreciation available. Compound annual growth (CAGR) is computed consistently for every pair; the source sheet's stated annual figure is shown for reference.

Methodology & Definitions

Source

Two sources. (1) Park City MLS fractional-interest sold export ("Fractional MLS Export Cleaned v2"), 244 closed sales, July 2023 – July 2026, covering 12 subdivisions/programs; every record includes sold price, SF, bedrooms, share fraction, and days on market. (Pinnacle is excluded from this analysis.) (2) A Colorado repeat-sale compilation ("Fractional Share Appreciation") covering 9 programs in Vail, Beaver Creek, Steamboat, and Aspen/Snowmass; the most recent sale of each of its 22 pairs is included in the analysis and explorer statistics, plus additional verified closings from listing-record research: a second Solaris Vail sale, five Residences at The Little Nell sales, four One Steamboat Place sales (RC-309-V, RC-403-I, RC-405-IV, and 1/12 interest RC-513-VII), and Gravity Haus Vail P-5 ($325,000, 1/31/2022), 2022–2026. Both sales of each repeat pair drive the Appreciation tab. Colorado bedrooms, bathrooms, usage weeks, and reservation systems were researched per unit from listing portals (Zillow, Redfin, Compass, brokerage IDX), club sites, and SherpaReport; where a specific interest could not be matched, the floor-plan value for that SF stack is used, and one Little Nell sale remains unmatched (fields blank). DOM is populated only for the 5 Colorado sales with verifiable MLS listing history (two Ritz-Carlton Vail sales at 451 and 497 days, Little Nell F-410 at 408 days list-to-contract across relists, Park Hyatt Week 52 at ~128 days, Timbers Club Snowmass at ~118 days); all other Colorado DOM is blank rather than estimated. Colorado rows are excluded from the Park City-only trend chart. Colorado programs were assigned to ownership models as follows: Four Seasons, Ritz-Carlton, Park Hyatt, Solaris, One Steamboat Place, Timbers Club, and The Little Nell as private residence clubs; Gravity Haus as club community shared ownership; 1592 Golf Terrace as Pacaso. Every record includes sold price, total residence SF, bedrooms, share fraction, and days on market; The Park City file has no missing values and every stated sold $/SF reconciles to sold price ÷ SF.

Definitions

Share $/SF = share sold price ÷ total SF of the underlying residence. Whole-home-equivalent $/SF = share $/SF × share denominator (e.g., a 1/8 share at $237/SF implies $1,898/SF if all eight shares sold at that rate); this measures how much total value a fractional structure extracts from one residence. Price per usage week = share price ÷ approximate annual usage weeks for that program. Medians are used throughout because prices are right-skewed.

Controlling for share size

Raw share prices are never compared across fraction types: a 1/4 of a home will always cost more than a 1/8 of the same home, so the "median share price" chart measures entry price and affordability only. Cross-model and cross-size value comparisons use normalized metrics: whole-home-equivalent $/SF multiplies each sale's $/SF by its share denominator, putting a $300,000 quarter-share and a $150,000 eighth-share of the same residence on an identical scale; price per usage week normalizes by the annual time received; and the Appreciation tab uses repeat sales of the same share, where the denominator cancels out entirely. Whole-home-equivalent assumes all shares of a residence clear at similar $/SF: true within a stack of identical shares, though in practice premium weeks or seasons can trade above standard shares.

Caveats

1) All 1/12 and 1/24 sales are at the Deer Valley Club (opened 1992), so small-share statistics reflect that building's age as much as the share size. 2) The Residences at the Chateaux advertises "unlimited, flexible usage" under club policies; 8.7 weeks (1/6 of a year) is used as the calendar-equivalent. 3) Pacaso's 44 nights ≈ 6.3 weeks. 4) 2026 is a partial year. 5) Reservation rules summarized from public listing remarks and operator documentation (Pacaso scheduling FAQ); verify against governing documents before relying on them contractually.
