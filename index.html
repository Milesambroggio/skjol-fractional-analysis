SWOT by Ownership Model

Five structural models operate in this comp set. Each program was classified by three tests: WHO operates it (tech platform, hospitality operator, resort hotel, private club, or the owners themselves), WHAT the physical product is (home, club condo, or hotel room), and HOW owners receive time (floating nights, reserved weeks + space-available, pure rotation, selected weeks, or fixed weeks). Definitions and assignments below; SWOT statistics are computed from the sales data.

Model definitions & property assignments

Pacaso co-ownership. A third-party technology platform owns the management layer. Standalone luxury homes; time is floating nights booked by app (approx. 44/yr per 1/8), no weeks at all; rentals prohibited. Properties: The Aerie, Old Town homes, SkyRidge, The Ridge at Canyons Village, 1592 Golf Terrace (Vail).
Private residence club. A hospitality operator runs a club of residences with hotel-grade services (concierge, daily housekeeping); usage is reserved seasonal weeks plus space-available overflow. The industry's dominant structure. Properties: Victory Ranch, The Residences at the Chateaux, Deer Valley Club; Colorado: Four Seasons Vail, Ritz-Carlton Vail, Park Hyatt Beaver Creek, Timbers Club Snowmass, One Steamboat Place, The Little Nell, Solaris Vail, Gravity Haus Vail.
Hotel quarter-share. Condo-hotel rooms (not club residences) sold in quarters on a simple rotating calendar, roughly a week per month, with a rental pool for unused time. No reserved seasonal weeks. Properties: Grand Summit Hotel, Canyon Residences.
Club community shared ownership. The value driver is bundled membership in a private club community; the product is a whole home. Selected weeks only: no space-available system, no hotel services. Properties: Tuhaye (Talisker Club Morningstar).
Self-managed fixed weeks. No operator brand: a small owner group holding fixed deeded weeks, often with rental and trade freedom. Properties: Sky Lodge, Red Stag Lodge, Pinnacle (PinClub).

One judgment call: Solaris is night-based like Pacaso but is grouped with residence clubs for its managed rental pool and service level. Gravity Haus Vail is classified as a private residence club per its service model (daily housekeeping, valet, club amenities with fixed + floating weeks).

Share Appreciation — Repeat Sales

Compound annual growth (CAGR) is computed consistently for every pair; the source sheet's stated annual figure is shown for reference.

Methodology & Definitions

Source

Two sources. (1) Park City MLS fractional-interest sold export ("Fractional MLS Export Cleaned v2"), 245 closed sales, July 2023 – July 2026, covering 13 subdivisions/programs; every record includes sold price, SF, bedrooms, share fraction, and days on market. (2) A Colorado repeat-sale compilation ("Fractional Share Appreciation") covering 9 programs in Vail, Beaver Creek, Steamboat, and Aspen/Snowmass; the most recent sale of each of its 22 pairs is included in the overview, SWOT, and explorer statistics, and both sales of each pair drive the Appreciation tab. Colorado bedrooms, bathrooms, usage weeks, and reservation systems were researched per unit from listing portals (Zillow, Redfin, Compass, brokerage IDX), club sites, and SherpaReport; where a specific interest could not be matched, the floor-plan value for that SF stack is used, and one Little Nell sale remains unmatched (fields blank). DOM is populated only for the 5 Colorado sales with verifiable MLS listing history (two Ritz-Carlton Vail sales at 451 and 497 days, Little Nell F-410 at 408 days list-to-contract across relists, Park Hyatt Week 52 at ~128 days, Timbers Club Snowmass at ~118 days); all other Colorado DOM is blank rather than estimated. Colorado rows are excluded from the Park City-only trend chart. Colorado programs were assigned to ownership models as follows: Four Seasons, Ritz-Carlton, Park Hyatt, Solaris, One Steamboat Place, Timbers Club, and The Little Nell as private residence clubs; Gravity Haus as club community shared ownership; 1592 Golf Terrace as Pacaso. Every record includes sold price, total residence SF, bedrooms, share fraction, and days on market; The Park City file has no missing values and every stated sold $/SF reconciles to sold price ÷ SF.

Definitions

Share $/SF = share sold price ÷ total SF of the underlying residence. Whole-home-equivalent $/SF = share $/SF × share denominator (e.g., a 1/8 share at $237/SF implies $1,898/SF if all eight shares sold at that rate); this measures how much total value a fractional structure extracts from one residence. Price per usage week = share price ÷ approximate annual usage weeks for that program. Medians are used throughout because prices are right-skewed.

Controlling for share size

Raw share prices are never compared across fraction types: a 1/4 of a home will always cost more than a 1/8 of the same home, so the "median share price" chart measures entry price and affordability only. Cross-model and cross-size value comparisons use normalized metrics: whole-home-equivalent $/SF multiplies each sale's $/SF by its share denominator, putting a $300,000 quarter-share and a $150,000 eighth-share of the same residence on an identical scale; price per usage week normalizes by the annual time received; and the Appreciation tab uses repeat sales of the same share, where the denominator cancels out entirely. Whole-home-equivalent assumes all shares of a residence clear at similar $/SF: true within a stack of identical shares, though in practice premium weeks or seasons can trade above standard shares.

Caveats

1) All 1/12 and 1/24 sales are at the Deer Valley Club (opened 1992), so small-share statistics reflect that building's age as much as the share size. 2) Pinnacle's club calendar is not published; 8 weeks is an estimate for a 1/6 interest and its single sale should be treated as anecdote. 3) The Residences at the Chateaux advertises "unlimited, flexible usage" under club policies; 8.7 weeks (1/6 of a year) is used as the calendar-equivalent. 4) Pacaso's 44 nights ≈ 6.3 weeks. 5) 2026 is a partial year. 6) Reservation rules summarized from public listing remarks and operator documentation (Pacaso scheduling FAQ); verify against governing documents before relying on them contractually.
